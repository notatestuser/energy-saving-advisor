Lighting

Light Emitting Diode (LED)

Typically 8W
50,000 hours
£5.95 per bulb

Previously considered to be quite pricey for a light bulb, LED lamps are gaining traction as inexpensive replacements for their less efficient counterparts. Their light is whiter and much more ecologically friendly.

Fluorescent

Typically 23W
10,000 hours
£3.99 per bulb

Though strange in appearance after the long legacy of the incandescent, the fluorescent bulb was the first to bring cheap and efficient lighting to the home. It is now beginning to lose its reputation as an energy saver.

Incandescent

Typically 100W
750 hours
£0.45 per bulb

With its screw fitting named after Thomas Edison, the incandescent lamp was where it all started. These days most incandescent bulbs convert less than 5% of the energy they use into visible light.

Electronics

Liquid Crystal Display

~ 230 kWh annually

LCD displays, or "flat panels", are better looking and slightly more power efficient than their CRT predecessor. However, they are more costly to produce in the factory.

Cathode Ray Tube (CRT)

~ 240 kWh annually

CRT screens are the older "box" style of television. While their production actually releases less greenhouse gases than the production of an LCD, they are marginally less power efficient.

Plasma Display

~ 313 kWh annually

Plasma screens often take a similar "flat" form factor to LCD screens. They are called "plasma" displays because the technology utilises small cells containing gases.

Standby mode

Switched off

Uses no power

Few people have the time to switch off all of their devices after they've finished using them. But not to worry! There are cheap solutions on the market to help, such as plug-in timers.

Standby mode

Varies by device

If you think your digital set-top-box is not influencing your footprint while in standby, think again! The UK uses enough standby power to power 400,000 homes -- just so that the TV switches on that couple of seconds faster!

Always on

Full power consumption

Modern devices are typically smart enough to switch themselves off after a period of inactivity, but for audio-visual equipment like set-top-boxes it's difficult to know when that is. Many devices will keep themselves powered up in order to avoid disturbing their viewer.
